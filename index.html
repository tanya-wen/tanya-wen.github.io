Tanya Wen


Cognitive neuroscience researcher

I am a cognitive neuroscientist dedicated to answering questions about human brain and behavior. I use a variety of tools, including behavioral measures, EEG/MEG, and fMRI to answer questions about how humans attend to relevant targets, represent task rules, task structure, and how we can flexibility adjust our cognitive control processes to meet task demands.

I earned a bachelor's degree in psychology and double-majored in life sciences at National Cheng Kung University in Taiwan between 2011-2015. Later, I earned my Ph.D. in medical science from the University of Cambridge from 2015-2019, under the supervision of Dr. John Duncan and Dr. Daniel Mitchell at the MRC Cognition and Brain Sciences Unit. I am currently a postdoctoral associate working with Dr. Tobias Egner at the Center for Cognitive Neuroscience at Duke University.
